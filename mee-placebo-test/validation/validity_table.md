## Supplementary Table S1: Placebo design validity metrics

Metrics quantifying the validity of negative-control (placebo) designs across three case studies. Spatial and temporal overlaps are Pearson correlations of log-transformed record counts. Geographic overlap is the Jaccard index of occupied grid cells. Recorder overlap is the proportion of unique contributor IDs shared between main and diagnostic contexts. Values are based on simulated data calibrated to published characteristics of each data source.

| Metric | Case 1: Dawn chorus | Case 2: Insect ALAN | Case 3: Fragmentation |
|--------|--------------------:|--------------------:|----------------------:|
| **Mechanism exclusion** | Strong | Strong | Moderate–Strong |
| Spatial overlap (*r*) | 0.82 (Strong) | 0.76 (Strong) | 0.59† (Moderate) |
| Recorder / platform overlap | 0.58 (Moderate) | 1.00‡ (Strong) | 1.00‡ (Strong) |
| Temporal overlap (*r*) | 0.97 (Strong) | 0.99 (Strong) | 0.93† (Strong) |
| Geographic / compositional overlap | 0.49§ (Moderate) | 0.54 (Moderate) | 0.42† (Moderate) |
| **Overall validity** | **Strong** | **Strong** | **Moderate** |

† Mean across diagnostic taxa (grassland plants: *r*_sp = 0.57, *r*_temp = 0.98, geo = 0.43; dragonflies: *r*_sp = 0.62, *r*_temp = 0.88, geo = 0.42).

‡ Platform identity: both main and diagnostic taxa sourced from GBIF (maximum possible platform overlap).

§ Jaccard index of species recorded in both dawn and midday windows.
